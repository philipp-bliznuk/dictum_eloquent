##### Quadrangle (noun)
*ˈkwä-ˌdraŋ-gəl*
- **a 4-sided enclosure especially when surrounded by buildings**
- **courtyard, patio, enclosure**
	since the weather was sunny, the convocation was held outside in the college's _quadrangle_

##### Quadrilateral (noun / adjective)
*ˌkwä-drə-ˈla-t(ə-)rəl*
- **a polygon of four sides**
- **having four sides**
	The two were, in a sense, trying to prove that shapes like these *quadrilaterals* don’t exist.
	If a *quadrilateral* sponge can live in a pineapple under the sea, a squirrel can wonder about the Moon Hoax.

##### Quagmire (noun)
*ˈkwag-ˌmī(-ə)r*
- **soft miry land that shakes or yields under the foot**
- **a difficult, precarious, or entrapping position: predicament**
	a protracted custody dispute that became a judicial _quagmire_
	the party was once again facing its quadrennial _quagmire_: the candidate sufficiently liberal to win the nomination would be too liberal for the general election

##### Qualm (noun)
*ˈkwälm*
- **a sudden attack of illness, faintness, or nausea**
- **implies an uneasy fear that one is not following one's conscience or better judgment**

	she has no _qualms_ about downloading pirated music files from the Internet

##### Quandary (noun)
*ˈkwän-d(ə-)rē*

- **dilemma**

	I'm in a _quandary_ about whether I should try to repair my stereo or buy a new one, even though I don't have the money to do either.

##### Quarry (noun / verb)
*ˈkwȯr-ē*

	a hunter relentlessly tracking his _quarry_

##### Quarterstaff (noun)
*ˈkwȯ(r)-tər-ˌstaf*
- **a long stout staff formerly used as a weapon and wielded with one hand in the middle and the other between the middle and the end**
	In the tales of Robin Hood, Little John famously defeats Robin in a duel on a log bridge using nothing but a sturdy *quarterstaff*.
	While swords were expensive status symbols, the *quarterstaff* was the practical weapon of choice for travelers and peasants because it was cheap to make and highly effective.

##### Quincentennial / Quincentenary (adjective / noun)
*ˌkwin-sen-ˈte-nē-əl* / *ˌkwin-sen-ˈte-nə-rē*

	Leonardo’s _quincentenary_ isn't just an opportunity to reassess his genius but, with the 500th anniversary of Raphael’s death in 2020, the start of two years in which to ponder the Renaissance.

##### Quorum (noun)
*ˈkwȯr-əm*

- **a select group**
	We need five people to make a _quorum_.
	In order to adopt articles, according to the rules, the assembly will need a *quorum* of two thirds.
