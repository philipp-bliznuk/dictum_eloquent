##### Quadrangle (noun)
*ˈkwä-ˌdraŋ-gəl*
- **a 4-sided enclosure especially when surrounded by buildings**
- **courtyard, patio, enclosure**
	since the weather was sunny, the convocation was held outside in the college's _quadrangle_

##### Quadrilateral (noun / adjective)
*ˌkwä-drə-ˈla-t(ə-)rəl*
- **a polygon of four sides**
- **having four sides**
	The two were, in a sense, trying to prove that shapes like these *quadrilaterals* don’t exist.
	If a *quadrilateral* sponge can live in a pineapple under the sea, a squirrel can wonder about the Moon Hoax.

##### Quagmire (noun)
*ˈkwag-ˌmī(-ə)r*
- **soft miry land that shakes or yields under the foot**
- **a difficult, precarious, or entrapping position: predicament**
	a protracted custody dispute that became a judicial _quagmire_
	the party was once again facing its quadrennial _quagmire_: the candidate sufficiently liberal to win the nomination would be too liberal for the general election

##### Quail (verb)
*ˈkwāl*
- **to give way: falter**
- **to recoil in dread or terror: cower**
	Other politicians _quailed_ before him.
	He _quailed_ at the thought of seeing her again.

##### Quaint (adjective)
*ˈkwānt*
- **suggests an old-fashioned but pleasant oddness**
- **marked by skillful design**
	a _quaint_ fishing village
	*quaint* with many a device in India ink

##### Qualm (noun)
*ˈkwälm*
- **a sudden attack of illness, faintness, or nausea**
- **implies an uneasy fear that one is not following one's conscience or better judgment**
	The doctor seemed seized with a *qualm* of faintness.
	she has no _qualms_ about downloading pirated music files from the Internet

##### Quandary (noun)
*ˈkwän-d(ə-)rē*
- **a state of perplexity or doubt**
- **dilemma**
	The unexpected results of the test have created a _quandary_ for researchers.
	I'm in a _quandary_ about whether I should try to repair my stereo or buy a new one, even though I don't have the money to do either.

##### Quarry (noun / verb)
*ˈkwȯr-ē*
- **one that is sought or pursued: prey**
- **an open excavation usually for obtaining building stone, slate, or limestone**
	a hunter relentlessly tracking his _quarry_
	an area where workers are _quarrying_ for limestone

##### Queasy (adjective)
*ˈkwē-zē*
- **causing nausea**
- **full of doubt: hazardous**
	He feels _queasy_ about taking the test.
	She had the _queasy_ feeling that she was being watched.

##### Quell (verb)
*ˈkwel*
- **to thoroughly overwhelm and reduce to submission or passivity**
- **quiet, pacify**
	the National Guard was called in to help _quell_ the late-night disturbances downtown
	the principal held up her hand to _quell_ the students so they could hear the urgent announcement

##### Quest (noun / verb)
*ˈkwest*
- **an act or instance of seeking**
- **to hunt, to demand, to seek**
	They were _questing_ for gold.
	I respectfully _quest_ your assistance in this matter.

##### Quincunx (noun)
*ˈkwin-ˌkəŋ(k)s*
- **an arrangement of five things in a square or rectangle with one at each corner and one in the middle**
	At the same time, Saturn continues its tense square to Uranus while making a *quincunx* to the full moon.
	We may be tempted to push ourselves to extremes on Tuesday, as the Sun creates a *quincunx* with optimistic Jupiter.
