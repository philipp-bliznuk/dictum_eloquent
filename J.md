##### Jaded (adjective)
*ˈjā-dəd*
- **fatigued by overwork: exhausted**
- **made dull, apathetic, or cynical by experience or by having or seeing too much of something; seasoned**
	He became _jaded_ from years of work as a police officer.
	a public _jaded_ by political scandals

##### Jamb (noun)
*ˈjam*
- **an upright piece or surface forming the side of an opening (as for a door, window, or fireplace)**
- **a projecting columnar part or mass**
	Sealing up door _jambs_, window seals and other entry points, as well as placing screens on windows and doors, can also be effective.
	If the weather is too cold, the water risks getting into the door _jambs_ or handles, leaving you unable to open your doors until the car is defrosted.

##### Jar (verb)
*ˈjär*
- **to have a harshly disagreeable or disconcerting effect**
- **to undergo severe vibration**
	The noise _jarred_ on her nerves.
	The earthquake _jarred_ the tiles loose.

##### Jaunty (adjective)
*ˈjȯn-tē*
- **sprightly in manner or appearance: lively**
- **stylish**
	He struck a _jaunty_ pose for the camera.
	She was wearing a _jaunty_ white cap.

##### Jell (verb)
*ˈjel*
- **to take shape and achieve distinctness: become cohesive**
- **to come to the consistency of jelly: congeal, set**
	Our plans are finally starting to _jell_.
	Boil the jam until it begins to _jell_.

##### Jetty (noun / verb)
*ˈje-tē*
- **a structure extended into a sea, lake, or river to influence the current or tide or to protect a harbor: pier**
- **project, jut**
	didn't see any passengers waiting for the ferry, so the captain sailed past the _jetty_
	Stay off of _jetties_, piers, rocks, and other waterside infrastructure.

##### Jink (verb / noun)
*ˈjiŋk*
- **to move quickly or unexpectedly with sudden turns and shifts (as in dodging)**
- **a quick evasive turn: slip**
	_jinking_ here and there with remarkable agility, the thieving urchin frustrated all attempts to catch him
	But maturation is a funny thing for a singer whose generous high *jinks* are foundational to his popularity.

##### Jock (noun)
*ˈjäk*
- **a person devoted to a single pursuit or interest**
- **pilot (especially: a fighter pilot)**
- **athlete (especially: a school or college athlete)**
	computer *jocks*
	Sports *jocks* and computer nerds spent a lifetime at odds with each other.

##### Jodhpur (noun)

##### Juxtapose (verb)
